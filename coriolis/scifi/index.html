Artificial gravity in science fiction

Artificial gravity has never been used in space, so our current perspective comes from science fiction. Sci-fi movies are not realistic; a “real” picture is not cinematic and would be difficult to simulate. No space movie has ever depicted the Coriolis effect.

In many sci-fi movies, you see an artificial gravity “generator” underneath the station that uses an energy source to produce pure gravity. This is impossible!

Some movies have taken a more “realistic” approach to sci-fi, showing rotating space stations. I will discuss some of these stories below. There are many options to choose from.

Sci-fi films with rotating spacecraft

Here is a short list of sci-fi films with rotating spacecraft, and the names of those spacecraft:

  • 2001: A Space Odyssey (1968): Spacecraft Discovery One

  • Ender’s Game (2013): Battle School

  • Interstellar (2014): Endurance

  • The Martian (2015): Hermes

  • Passengers (2016): the Avalon

Even in films that take a more realistic approach, there are some mistakes in their gravity simulations. Here are a few examples.

The Avalon

In Passengers, the entire story takes place aboard a rotating spacecraft, the Avalon. Passengers travel for many years to emigrate to another star system. In this movie, you see correctly that a ride on the elevator could temporarily cause 0g. One mistake is that a failure in the gravity drive would not cause gravity to “stop” abruptly; the station’s rotational momentum would prevent that.

Ender’s Game is a fantasy story, but the main plot is about artificial gravity and weightlessness. Ender is trained to fight weightless space battles in a zero-g “battle room.” In this “battle room,” every action Ender takes depends on his direction. This would also be true in artificial gravity due to the Coriolis effect. One missing gravity detail is that the hallway into the battle room would be almost zero-g. Gravity would vary based on how far you are from the center of rotation.

It seems clear that an accurate spacecraft story requires a physics expert. We simply do not have experience with such things to know clearly what it would be like.
