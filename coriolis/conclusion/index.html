Conclusion

Artificial gravity will be difficult to accomplish, though it will also be essential to space travel. If we were to move around in a spinning space station, we would feel unexpected “forces” acting on us. An artificial gravity habitat could be built in the near future with the right designs in place. For now, the Coriolis effect gives us something to think about. Now we know how it would work.

Another Coriolis model

This website contains another interesting Coriolis model. Here, Andy Giger poses a similar question: what does it look like if you have spinning water jets? As you can see in his model, the water veers forward, into the direction of rotation. If you look carefully, you will see that every dot individually follows a perfectly straight line.

My experience with physics

I would quickly like to note my experience with physics. I do not have a physics degree, but I have studied physics-mechanics three times in school. I was successful each time. Physics fascinates me!

I originally studied physics as a high school student. The next year, I studied physics again as an advanced placement student.

In college, I ended up taking this course a third time due to some school requirements. I took “Principles of Physics I, for Scientists and Engineers.” I earned an A.

In none of these classes was the Coriolis effect ever mentioned. I was never given a physics problem similar to this one. It seems too complicated for an exam. I cannot remember where I originally read about the Coriolis effect. This subject is something that has fascinated me for years.
